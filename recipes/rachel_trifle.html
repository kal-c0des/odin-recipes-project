Rachel's Thanksgiving Trifle

An iconic holiday food, turned even more iconic-er!

If I learned anything from Friends, it's that sticky magazine pages can lead to new and
interesting recipes! Rachel's English Trifle is one to remember. It's so good, even the
birds love it. Don't be like Chandler - if you eat in on the balcony, hold on to your plate
or they just might fly off with it and drop it in the street!

Be prepared to call your friends to tell them how good it is (from your kid's room)..
Heck, take it in the bathroom so you can look at it while you eat it!
Wherever you decide to eat it, just enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • 10 pcs - Ladyfingers
  • 8 oz - Jam (strawberry or raspberry)
  • 1 box - Custard mix
  • 12 oz - Raspberries
  • 10 pcs - More ladyfingers
  • 1 lb - Ground beef
  • 1 can - Peas
  • 1 - Onion (diced)
  • 2 pc - Bananas (sliced)
  • 1.5 cups - Whipped cream

Recipe Instructions:

  1. Heat a large pan and add a bit of cooking oil.
  2. Add the ground beef to the pan and cook until slightly browned.
  3. In a separate pan, sauté onions until golden then add the peas.
  4. Mix in the peas and onions and cook until beef is browned.
  5. Drain any excess oil and set the beef mixture aside to cool.
  6. Prepare custard according to the instructions on the box and set aside.
    7. (You can also make it from scratch if that's something you're into)
  7. In a glass trifle bowl, begin by placing a layer of ladyfingers at the bottom.
  8. Add a heaping layer of jam over the ladyfingers and spread evenly.
  9. Add half of your custard mixture and layer evenly over the jam.
  10. Place a layer of raspberries neatly onto your custard.
  11. Add another layer of ladyfingers.
  12. Pour your sautéed beef, peas, and onions over the ladyfingers. Spread even.
  13. Add your last layer of custard.
  14. Cut bananas into thin slices and place them on the custard layer. Make it pretty!
  15. Top with whipped cream and serve.
